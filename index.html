My darling Jake and Rose,
         I’ve gotten this website together for you, because when I Googled myself, I found that after forty plus years of writing for magazines and newspapers, only three items showed up on line. The first was the only bad review of my book, Can This Be Love?, a pathologically nasty piece of prose by an anonymous Kirkus reviewer, who made such off-target assessments of my personal life that I can only guess he or she was looking at it through the lens of his or her own misery. The second was one of the two pieces I wrote for The New York Times. (The other one had gone missing, but you’ll find it here.) And the third was “The Myth of the Male Orgasm,” which I originally wrote for The Village Voice, but which was reprinted in several other places and included in both Titters, The First Collection of Humor by Women, and a college textbook for a women’s studies course. (Go figure!)
         And that, essentially, was it. The dozens and dozens of pieces I wrote for Ms., Glamour, Family Circle, Redbook, McCall’s, New Woman, The East Hampton Star, Parenting, The Daily News, Child, Sesame Street Magazine, Health, etc., never made it onto the www. And so many of them, as you’ll see, were about you—your triumphs and shenanigans and all the ways you made my life so wonderful.
         The site is not complete, unfortunately—because I can’t find every tear sheet I could have sworn I’d kept—but it should give you an idea of what I was doing all those times when you were growing up and came to my closed office door asking for one thing or another (money for something, permission for something, spelling help) only to hear me yell, “Not now, Mommy’s working!”
All my love,
Mom
